Tertiary Prevention

What is tertiary prevention?
Tertiary prevention, also known as clinical prevention, refers to the timely and effective treatment measures taken to prevent the deterioration of the disease, prevent complications and disability;For those who have lost their work force or disability, the main purpose is to promote functional recovery, psychological rehabilitation and home nursing guidance, so that the patients can regain their living and working ability as far as possible, participate in social activities and prolong their life.
The main measures of tertiary prevention
The main measures of tertiary prevention include symptomatic treatment and rehabilitation:
Symptomatic treatment can improve the symptoms, reduce pain, reduce the adverse reactions of the disease, prevent complications and disability, strive for injury but not disabled, has lost the labor force or disabled through rehabilitation treatment, can promote their physical and mental recovery, restore the labor force, strive for disability but not invalid, protect their ability to live.
The measures of rehabilitation treatment include functional rehabilitation, psychological rehabilitation, social rehabilitation and vocational rehabilitation.
