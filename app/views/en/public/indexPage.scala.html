Public DataBase

TCGA Database

TCGA, The Cancer Genome Atlas, is a Cancer Research project jointly established by The National Cancer Institute (NCI) and The National Human Genome Research Institute (NHGRI). It provides a large, free reference database for Cancer Research by collecting and sorting various omics data related to Cancer.

cBioPortal Database

CBioPortal contains genetic data types including somacellular mutations, CHANGES in DNA copy number,mRNA and microRNA expression,DNA methylation, protein abundance and phosphorus protein abundance. To date,cBioPortal contains 10 published tumor research results and more than 20 TCGA results for each tumor sample and data that can be downloaded from the websiteCBioPortal these complex data consolidated and simplified from gene level, each sample can query the specific biological characteristics, such as gene mutations, homozygous absence, gene amplification, mRNA and increase or decrease of micrornas, protein increased or decreased etc. This allows researchers to query each gene and sample genes change, verify some fake.

Oncomine Database

Oncomine is currently the world's largest database of cancer gene chips and an integrated data mining platform designed to mine cancer genetic information. Oncomine has the most complete cancer mutation profile, gene expression data, and relevant clinical information that can be used to identify new biomarkers or new therapeutic targets. Oncomine combines RNA and DNA-SEQ data from GEO, TCGA and published literature sources.

SEER Database

SEER database, the U.S. national cancer institute, "surveillance, epidemiology and result database" SEER in North America is one of the most representative large tumor registration database, collected a large number of relevant data of evidence-based medicine, evidence-based practice for clinicians and clinical medical research provides evidence to support and valuable first-hand information. Clinical data from the SEER database includes patient registration Numbers, personal information, location of the primary lesion, tumor size, tumor code, treatment, cause of death, and more.
